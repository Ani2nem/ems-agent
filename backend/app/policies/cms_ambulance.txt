CMS / MEDICARE - Ambulance Services Medical Necessity (public reference excerpt)

Coverage principle
  Medicare covers ambulance transport only when using any other means of
  transportation would endanger the patient's health. The patient's condition
  at the time of transport must require the vehicle and the level of service
  billed. Coverage is condition-based, not diagnosis-based.

Levels of service
  BLS (Basic Life Support): transport plus basic, non-invasive interventions.
  ALS (Advanced Life Support): required when the patient's condition warrants an
  ALS assessment or one or more ALS interventions (e.g., cardiac monitoring,
  IV access with medication administration, advanced airway management). An ALS
  assessment performed by ALS-qualified personnel to determine the patient's
  need for an ALS intervention supports an ALS-level claim even if no ALS
  intervention is ultimately furnished, provided the dispatch was warranted by
  the reported condition.

Medical necessity indicators
  Emergent (Priority 1) dispatch, altered mental status (GCS < 15), unstable
  vitals, significant mechanism of injury, or the need for monitoring or
  medications en route support medical necessity for emergency ambulance
  transport at the assessed level of service.

Common denial reasons
  CO-50  Non-covered: service not deemed medically necessary for the condition.
  CO-16  Claim lacks information required for adjudication.
  CO-11  Diagnosis inconsistent with the procedure/level billed.

Downcoding
  When documentation supports transport but not the ALS level billed, the payer
  may reimburse at the BLS level (DOWNGRADE) rather than deny outright.
