AETNA - Ambulance Services Clinical Policy Bulletin (public reference excerpt)

Medical necessity
  Aetna considers emergency ambulance transport medically necessary when the
  member's condition is such that any other method of transport is
  contraindicated, and transport is to the nearest facility able to treat the
  condition. Advanced Life Support (ALS) is covered when the member's reported
  or observed condition requires ALS assessment or ALS-level interventions.

ALS-supporting criteria
  Cardiac monitoring, IV access with medication administration, advanced airway
  management, or evidence of hemodynamic or neurologic instability. A high-acuity
  dispatch (Priority 1 / emergent) together with a significant mechanism of
  injury or an abnormal presentation (e.g., GCS < 15, hypotension, hypoxia)
  supports the ALS level of service.

Documentation
  The patient care report should describe the condition that required transport,
  the interventions performed en route, and why a lower level of transport would
  have endangered the member. Contemporaneous vitals and a clear narrative are
  expected.

Adjudication codes
  CO-50  Not medically necessary for the documented condition.
  CO-16  Missing or incomplete information.
  CO-11  Diagnosis inconsistent with the level of service billed.
  DOWNGRADE  Transport supported at BLS rather than the ALS level submitted.
